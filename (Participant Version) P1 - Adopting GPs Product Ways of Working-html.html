What is a Product Operating Model?

 

A framework of principles and practices that guide how products are built, how problems are solved, and which problems we solve 

With our current operating model, we face

 

 

Current State

 

•

High churn: Above-benchmark rate of wasted output 

due to lack of direct customer engagement

 

•

Lack of Customer-Centricity: Project-led mindset 

that prioritizes short-term project delivery over 

long-term customer value

•

Siloed teams: Frequent handoffs and disjointed 

communication between product and tech

 

•

Near-term Focus: Funding model incentivizes 

short-term revenue rather than innovation

We are shifting to a new product operating model 

deﬁned by 3 core principles

Desired State

 

Customer-Centric

Product-Led

Cross-Functional

A Day in the Life of a Product Manager – Before and After PWoW

She joins a product review where she presents 

the product brief. They align on roadmap 

milestones based on internal stakeholder 

priorities

Sophie starts her day sorting through many 

messages and emails trying to align with Ops, 

Legal, and Sales on a conﬂicting feature request

Today

Tomorrow – Product Ways of Working

Sophie spends an hour writing up multiple 

updates for different stakeholders – each asking 

for the same thing in slightly different formats

An ask from a senior stakeholder strays from the 

roadmap. Sophie meets with the Engineering 

team who is frustrated with the ad-hoc requests

Sophie and the Engineering + UX Leads 

huddle using their OKR dashboard. Alignment is 

quick since priorities were set in quarterly planning

Sophie shares recent user insights to frame the 

discussion. Stakeholders weigh in on tradeoffs and 

prioritize using data-driven decisions

Sophie can relay updates published via a shared 

dashboard across teams. Stakeholders can 

review and comment independently

Roadmap risks were ﬂagged in an earlier PDLC 

phase, so the team was prepared to adjust – They 

determine that scope can be built into the next 

sprint

Overview of PDLC

The next key change in our Processes is how we execute the Product Development Life Cycle (PDLC)

Guiding principles of our target-state PDLC:

Be Iterative

Minimize bureaucracy and empower sub-domains and pods with local 

ownership

Ensure Seamless Collaboration

Break silos between product, design, engineering, and delivery with clear 

handoffs and shared accountability

Adapt to Product Maturity

Tailor product pathways to product maturity and scale of change, 

guiding checkpoints

Drive Continuous Readiness

Operational readiness runs in parallel to requirements, roadmaps, 

prioritization and delivery

Use Checkpoints as Triggers

Trigger downstream processes, 

including Risk Review, Technical 

Architecture Committee (TAC) review, etc.

The target-state PDLC provides a clear 

structure for managing products 

end-to-end

While the PDLC sets expectations, it is not a 

set of rigid steps. It’s there to drive product 

outcomes and continuous improvement
