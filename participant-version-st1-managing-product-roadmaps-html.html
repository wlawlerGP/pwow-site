How to identify and sequence dependencies?

Having prioritized the right work, sequence it into deliverable outcomes to ensure your roadmap & MVP plan remain realistic and achievable

Dependencies deﬁne the order of operations for 

delivering value, helping teams manage speed, cost, 

and learning

•

Ensure cross-team alignment across pods, domains, 
and platforms

•

Surface risks and blockers early

•

Balance priorities and release timing realistically

1. Map Dependencies

Identify upstream, downstream, and 

cross-domain relationships

2. Evaluate Items

Score by:

• Customer value / OKR impact

• Feasibility / readiness

• Dependency risk

3. Sequence Accordingly

set realistic delivery timelines

• Slot dependent work after enabling items

•

Re-sequence quarterly via QBRs or sprint planning

MVP vs. Milestone

Milestone - A dated checkpoint that 

proves a claim and unlocks a decision

MVP - The smallest, shippable 

experiment with clear success/stop 

criteria

MVPs are the smallest steps that generate 

evidence toward them

o Deliver early value to a speciﬁc persona
o Validate a stated hypothesis with target 

metrics

o Reduce risk (market, usability, viability, 

technical)

o Inform the roadmap with evidence → pivot / 

persevere / stop

Milestones & Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

Milestones set decision gates. MVPs create the evidence to pass them

MVP card

For [persona/segment] who [problem],
we believe that enabling [target behavior] via [capability] will [expected change].
Success is proven when [metric] moves from X to target Y within Z weeks.
Decision: pivot / persevere / stop.
Kill switch: pre-agreed condition to stop.
